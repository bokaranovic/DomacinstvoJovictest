Mućeni pekmez — tradicija koja se ne kuva

Domaćinstvo Jović · april 2026. · 5 min čitanjaDomaćinstvo Jović · April 2026 · 5 min read

Svaki kraj ima svoj specijalitet po kojem ga pamte. Drvar ima mućeni pekmez od drenjine — a naša porodica ima čast da tu tradiciju čuva i dijeli sa svijetom.

Pekmez koji vatru nije vidio

Većina pekmeza nastaje satima na šporetu. Naš — nikada. Mućeni pekmez se pravi hladnim postupkom: probrana, prvoklasna drenjina se propasira, doda joj se šećer, i onda počinje ono najvažnije — mućenje. Puna tri sata, bez prestanka, dok se šećer potpuno ne sjedini s voćem i masa ne postane glatka poput svile.

Zato što se ne kuva, pekmez zadržava boju, miris i punoću svježe drenjine — i traje mjesecima bez ijednog konzervansa. Samo dren, šećer i tri sata ruku koje ne odustaju.

Ukus djetinjstva

Ko je jednom probao mućeni pekmez, kaže uvijek isto: „Ovo miriše na kuću moje bake." Jarkocrven, kiselkasto-sladak, gust ali mazav — najbolji je na toplom domaćem hljebu, uz kajmak, ili kašikom pravo iz tegle (nećemo nikome reći).

Od naše kuhinje do vašeg stola

Mućeni pekmez pravimo u malim serijama, onako kako se jedino i može — ručno. Zato ga nekad i nestane! Najsigurnije ćete ga uloviti ako ga naručite direktno — šaljemo ga poštom, pažljivo upakovanog, širom BiH i regiona.

Every region has a specialty it is remembered by. Drvar has its whipped cornelian pekmez — and our family has the honour of keeping that tradition alive and sharing it with the world.

The jam that never saw fire

Most jams spend hours on the stove. Ours — never. Whipped pekmez is made cold: first-class, hand-sorted cornelian cherries are strained, sugar is added, and then comes the most important part — whipping. Three full hours, without stopping, until the sugar fully merges with the fruit and the mass turns smooth as silk.

Because it is never cooked, the pekmez keeps the colour, aroma and fullness of the fresh berry — and lasts for months without a single preservative. Just dren, sugar, and three hours of hands that never give up.

The taste of childhood

Whoever tastes whipped pekmez once always says the same thing: "This smells like my grandmother's house." Bright red, sweet-and-sour, thick yet spreadable — best on warm homemade bread, with kajmak, or by the spoonful straight from the jar (we won't tell anyone).

From our kitchen to your table

We make whipped pekmez in small batches, the only way it can be made — by hand. That is why it sometimes runs out! The safest way to catch it is to order directly — we ship it by post, carefully packed, across Bosnia and the region.
